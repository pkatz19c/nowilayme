Blood. He could feel the warm, stickiness of the blood splattered across his left cheek, just under his eye. He felt one drop roll off his chin. It was not his blood. Instead of holding a bandaid, this time his mother held a knife. His father was lying on the floor, a scarlet puddle flowing quickly out from under him. Blood dripped slowly off the edge of the blade. His mother looked stunned. He screamed.
