“Take a seat anywhere you'd like, Hon, the others should be here in a moment. Would you like a glass of water?” The woman was gazing at her expectantly. Dawn wet her lips tentatively before speaking.

“Yes please.” The woman smiled, turned and fussed with the water cooler in the corner for a moment, and then returned, a white Styrofoam cup in her hand. Dawn took the proffered cup appreciatively.

Within the next few minutes, the three more chairs were filled, and the motherly-looking woman took her seat as well. No one spoke as the woman settled into her leather office chair, the soft squeaks of their hard plastic ones settling into silence as they all waiting for the woman to speak.

“Good afternoon, students, glad to see you all made it here alright. Today we have a new face joining our little group, so I think we'll start with introductions. I'll go first. My name is Dr. Dana Melody, I have a doctorate in psychology; I've been working here on campus for eight years now. I have suffered from insomnia since I was about fourteen years old.” The students mumbled brief hellos, all averting their gaze from one another. Dr. Melody gestured to her right, where Dawn sat rigidly. Seeing no way out of it, Dawn cleared her throat.

“Hi. I'm Dawn, I'm a Biology, Pre-Med student, and I have Sleep Apnea and Insomnia.” Getting it all out there was almost relieving to Dawn, and she shrank back into her chair, a little bit less tense, but still avoiding eye contact. The chorus of “Hi, Dawn”s did nothing to ease her embarrassment, and she wished someone else would speak quickly.

To her right, the dark-haired young man brushed back his hair and mumbled,

“I'm Quinton. I'm a Social Sciences major with an emphasis in Criminal Justice. I get nightmares, so I have Insomnia,” he finished lamely. He slouched back into his chair, his hair shielding his eyes once more.

Next, a blonde-haired girl leaned forward into the circle and gushed, “I'm Lindsey, I'm majoring in History, Chemistry, Music with an emphasis in vocal performance, and Business management. I'm also the President of the Baking club and the Math club, and I'm on the school newspaper staff. My professors recommended I come to counseling because…” she hesitated. “Well, I got in trouble for some stuff and it was this or getting removed from office,” she finished, smiling broadly, but her shoulders stiffened and her grin looked forced.

Finally, the last person spoke. His voice was scratchy and high pitched, and his face was twitching wildly as he spoke.

“I'm Luc. I'm here because I'm a Film Major and I haven't slept in three weeks.” He turned to look at Lindsey, and continued conversationally, “Too much editing work to do, you know? But my friends are getting worried that I'm gonna collapse on set or something, so they sent me here,” he laughed. Quinton flinched away from the sound as if Luc's amusement was physically painful for him. Lindsey continued to grin up at Luc, nodding her understanding. Dr. Melody cleared her throat and the room became silent once more.

“Okay,” she clapped her hands together. “Let's get started.”
