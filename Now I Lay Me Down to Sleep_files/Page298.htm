Quinton could feel their eyes like laser points focused on him. “Is that him?” someone nearby whispered. They passed the two girls before Quinton could hear the second girl's reply.

“...seemed like such a nice family…”

They passed a large group next. “...saw him on the news again last week. It's like the anniversary--” “His dad was murdered, right?” The guy stopped talking when he saw Quinton. A guy with a notepad and pencil at the ready had stopped to also engage in the conversation, but then, the journalism majors were always looking for gossip.

           The whispers continued. “It was awful. It was all over the news for months afterwards,” the first guy continued after Quinton had passed. “...cops dragged his mom out of the house…”  “...that's where she killed his dad. He was just standing…” “...was on the porch, watching as the cops drove her away…” “They say she ended up…”  “she's a permanent resident of the psych ward. Maybe it runs in the family…” Quinton was too wrapped up in eavesdropping on all the whispered conversations to focus on Lindsey. I didn’t get enough sleep to deal with this. He sighed, pushing his hair forward into his eyes again, trying to think of an excuse to get out of counseling that day. Nothing came to mind.
