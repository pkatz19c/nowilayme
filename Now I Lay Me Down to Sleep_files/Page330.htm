Lindsey dragged herself into her darkened bedroom as quietly as she could manage. It was 2 AM. After counseling, she’d gone to the mailroom to pick up a package, then she’d gone to the gym to workout for a while, and then to a meeting for the school newspaper. After that, she’d spent four hours working on a couple papers in the student center. She double-checked her day planner to make sure she’d checked off everything on her list. The entire page was littered with notes and check marks, but there was definitely no entry for Asian History. She frowned. With so many things going on, I’m bound to forget a few things here and there, but never have I forgotten something so important as the paper that was due in for Asian History... Her teacher had been kind enough to grant her an extension, but Lindsey recognized a dangerous pattern of behavior when she saw one. I haven't been spending enough time with my friends… I missed Taylor’s birthday. Undressing quietly in the dark, Lindsey simply dropped her things and climbed into bed. I’ll do better tomorrow. She laid on her back, staring up at the underside of the bunk bed her roommate had claimed, listening to her roommate's gentle snores. It was worth a shot.

 

Watch and guard me through the night…
