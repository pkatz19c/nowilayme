Dawn pulled the gray bag out from underneath her bed; pulled out the now familiar assortment of tubes and cords. Setting the main compartment on her bedside table gently, she hooked the end of one tube to it and fixed the other end to the clear plastic mask she had laid on the bed. Plugging the electrical cord first into the machine and then into the wall, Dawn waited for the CPAP machine to boot up. She filled the main chamber with distilled water and made a little hop onto her raised bed. Pulling the oxygen-style mask over her nose, Dawn took another deep, unassisted breath. Be brave, Dawn, be brave. Her roommate smiled over at her, still working away at her desk. Dawn smiled at her apologetically, but the older girl just shook her head and waved her goodnight. Dawn pulled a sleeping mask over her eyes to simulate darkness until her roommate went to sleep.

 

And wake me with the morning light.
