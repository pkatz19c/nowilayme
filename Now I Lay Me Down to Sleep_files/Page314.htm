Dr. Melody addressed the room at large. “Let's all share what we did to prepare for sleep last night. So you all got back to your rooms, maybe you watched TV or brushed your hair…” she trailed off, leaving the prompt just hanging in the air. Lindsey's broad grin seemed to freeze on her lips. She blinked slowly, once, twice, and showed her teeth as she seemed to force another smile.

           “Oh, you know there's always so many things going on at night! Like did you know that there's a sorority house on Wiltern Street that holds midnight madness pillow fights twice a week?! How could I miss that? And then while I was there, I overheard some girls talking about these guys who were gonna go into the woods on the edge of campus later to look for the secret tunnels that are rumored to run underneath the school. And as a reporter for the campus newspaper, I just couldn't turn down a scoop like that! I mean that could be the story that helps our little publication make it big!” Dr. Melody nodded her understanding, and waiting a moment after Lindsey finished before speaking.

“Okay, so you had a busy night. Around what time was it that you got back to your room?” she asked, evenly. Lindsey's enthusiasm dimmed slightly and she blinked again. “Around three this morning, I think,” she replied slowly. Instead of continuing to question Lindsey directly, Dr. Melody turned to scan the rest of the group.

“Did anyone else get back to their rooms late last night?” she asked evenly. Luc raised his hand and Dr. Melody smiled in his direction.

“How about you, Luc? What kept you out so late?” Luc smiled sheepishly, rubbing the back of his neck in a nervous gesture.

“We just finished filming for a midterm project, so I was in the lab late last night trying to get some editing done. We're trying to finish the initial project early so we can enter it in the film festival in town next week.” Dr. Melody nodded, looking impressed. “And how did it go? Did you get a lot of work done?” she asked, leaning forward, interested. Luc frowned.

“I didn't get as much work done as I'd hoped. And I kept dozing off or making mistakes on the editing because the energy drinks I took to keep me up made my hands shake so badly.” Quinton shifted in his seat slightly, as if in agreement. The movement did not escape Dr. Melody's notice.

“Quinton? How did it go for you last night? Did you get to sleep at all?” Quinton sat up, moving his hair out of his eyes. He shook his head slowly from side to side.

“I went to bed at 11. I did all the things we talked about last week. I made sure not to use electronics for 30 minutes before bed, I made sure the room was dark, and I took a hot shower before I got into bed. But I couldn't get to sleep.” It was the most anyone had heard him say since he'd entered the room, but Dr. Melody seemed to be waiting for a bit more. The silence stretched uncomfortably. The florescent lights above them hummed.

Eventually, she prompted, “And around what time do you think you fell asleep?” Quinton seemed to consider that for a moment.

“I fell asleep around 1:30,” he replied slowly. “I woke up around 3:30 and thought I'd gotten in like 2 hours. After that I just got up and went for a run.” Dr. Melody hesitated, but seemed to decide something.

“Was there a reason you woke at 3:30, or you just kind of woke up?” Quinton shielded his eyes again and mumbled, “Nightmares.” Dr. Melody didn't seem inclined to push him any further. She turned to Dawn instead.

“This is your first meeting, but I want you to know that this is a safe space. Anything you say here stays within this circle. You don't have to, but I would encourage you to share your experiences, because there might be someone else in this room who has experienced the same thing.” Dawn nodded and then took a deep breath, shutting her eyes tightly. She opened her eyes and stared straight at the ground as she spoke.

“I have Sleep Apnea. I got diagnosed over Winter break. I just got my CPAP machine, but... I'm afraid to go to sleep because I'm afraid I won't wake up.” There was dead silence in the room. Even the lights stopped making noises. Dawn could feel her face getting hotter and hotter with each heartbeat. The smile had slipped off Lindsey's face, Luc was staring at her, apparently dumbfounded, and even Quinton had looked up from his slouched position. Dr. Melody finally broke the tension.

“Well, Dawn, I don't think you're the only one in this room with that fear, or some variation of it. Am I right?” she asked, again opening her question up to the whole group. Three heads nodded their assent. Dawn smiled weakly; she wasn't alone. Dr. Melody glanced down at her watch.

“So, we have about five minutes. I think in light of what we've uncovered her today. we should try our best to tackle this fear first. Fear is a physiological response to a stimulus that our brains have perceived as dangerous, right?” Four heads nodded. “This shared fear, the fear of not waking up, is probably contributing to the insomnia you're all experiencing,” Dr. Melody continued stating the obvious. “But fear is an instinct, and sometimes it is in our best interest to rationalize ourselves through the situation instead of relying on the fear to do it for us. We all have the capability to control our behavior, so we're going to try to reason our way into eradicating this fear. A method that I have personally found very useful is a short mantra I say before I get into bed, to help me believe that I will get to sleep, and that I will wake up in the morning. I want you to use it all tonight as you prepare to go to sleep…”
