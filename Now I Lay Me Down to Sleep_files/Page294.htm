Quinton's eyes snapped open, his entire body coated with a thin sheen of sweat. He groaned, rolling over to stare at the numbers on digital clock at his bedside. 3:37 AM. At least I got a good 2 hours in tonight, he grimaced. Sitting up slowly, Quinton swung his legs over the side of the bed and stood, careful to avoid the creaky spots in his dorm floor. He dressed in basketball shorts and a semi-clean t-shirt; grabbing his coat and his shoes, he slipped out the door and down the hall. A nice long run would clear his head.

“Hey, how are you?” It was a standard greeting.

“Good, just tired,” Came the expected response.

“Me too. See you.” The two students separated, heading in opposite directions, she hurrying off to class, he wandering aimlessly across the college green.

Quinton had already had this conversation four times that morning. In fact, this was his 582nd identical conversation that week. It seemed like everyone on the college campus was tired, both students and professors alike.

But none of them are as tired as me, he thought to himself, a small, almost grudging, upward tilt to his lips. College is a place where people with all kinds of experiences come to get away for awhile, and maybe learn how to make money while they do it, he thought. “But the problem is, no matter how far you run, you can't outrun your shadows,” he murmured aloud. He paused and looked dramatically off into the distance, running a hand through his dark, shoulder-length hair.

“Hey Quinton. What were you mumbling about just now?” asked a voice from behind. Blinking, Quinton turned to look down at the girl.

“Hi, Lindsey, how are you?” Quinton muttered, ignoring her question. He prepared himself for the obligatory answer. Lindsey didn't disappoint.

“Good. Busy. Tired. How are you?” she asked, beaming up at him with perfectly white, straight teeth. Quinton pursed his lips.

“Fine, same,” he replied. They walked in silence for a few moments. Quinton looked away from Lindsey, turning his face in the opposite direction and shoving his hands into his pockets. Snippets of the whispered conversations going on around them stole his attention.
