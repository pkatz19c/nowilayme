“Hey how are you?” The bottle blonde cheerleader greeted Lindsey in passing.

“Fine. Tired. How are you?” Lindsey flashed the girl a toothy smile. The other girl didn’t respond.

Okay, it's 1:50pm now. It'll take me about 7 minutes to get to the counseling center at this pace. That gives me exactly three minutes to use the restroom before the session. The session is always 40 minutes long. If I leave right at 2:40, that gives me ten minutes extra to work on that poster for the Chemistry club before my next class. At 4pm I have a meeting for Debate club, and an interview with the S.A. president at 5:30 for the school newspaper. That gives me 30 minutes between Debate club and the interview to eat and read for my Asian History class at 6... I have to do well on the reading quiz today to maintain my A average in the class. That'll put me right on track for the 4.0 GPA goal I set for the semester…

Lindsey's mind ran faster and faster as she went over her afternoon schedule in her head for the 32nd time. Lindsey’s hand shook with excess energy and she bounced on the balls of her feet as she walked. There’s just so much to do! Thinking back, Lindsey wondered when she had become such a workaholic. It wasn't that she never got out, it was just that even getting out added stress to her carefully-planned schedule. I have to do more. College only lasts so long, and who knows when I'll have the chance to experience this kind of environment again? After all, they say college is the best time of your life, she reasoned with herself. Glancing around, Lindsey noticed of all the stares she and Quinton were receiving from the students they passed. Looking up at Quinton, Lindsey registered the troubled look on his face, his lips tightened and his hair covering his eyes again.

What should I do? Should I say something? Was there anything we said in therapy last week that might help? I wasn't even paying attention last week because I was thinking of ways to make the Baking club's pies sell faster, so we could make more money to go to that competition in the Spring, and then trying to figure out how I could make it to the competition because that's the same week as the Math club's colloquium on the benefits of incorporating calculus as early as 4th grade...God, I'm so tired…

By the time Lindsey had finally decided to just say something, Quinton had paused, taken a step forward, and pulled open the door to their campus counseling center. He disappeared through the doorway before Lindsey could manage a word.
