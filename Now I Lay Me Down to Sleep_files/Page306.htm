“Hello, Hon, how are you?” The woman behind the desk asked warmly.

“Oh fine, fine… Just tired,” Dawn replied automatically.

The counseling center was as horrid a place as it sounded to Dawn. It looked like a doctor's office, with the standard pile of magazines on the coffee table separating two worn-out chairs, sagging in some places and uncomfortably rigid-looking in others. The woman behind the front desk had given her a pitying smile when she'd signed in for the insomniacs group counseling session a few minutes ago. Scanning the room, Dawn found the darkest, most secluded corner of the room and leaned against the wall. She needed help; her fear of dying had kept her awake for far too long, and her body was beginning to suffer for it. She had lost her appetite, her skin had begun to look sallow, and she had deep, dark rings around her eyes that her industrial-strength concealer could no longer hide. I know the doctors said this machine would help; that I'd get used to it, like it even... But I can't help wondering: What if it fails? What if it slips off? That would be an awful way for them to find me, with tubes hanging out of my face, curled up under my covers. And what about my poor roommate? What if the noise wakes her up? I don't want her to hate me…

“Dawn?” The sound of her own name broke Dawn's revelry and she jumped. A short, matronly-looking woman in the office doorway, unlike from the woman behind the counter, gave her a genuine smile. "Dawn, right? We're meeting in one of the conference rooms today. Follow me," she turned and marched back through the doorway she had just exited, and Dawn was reminded of the nurses at the hospital leading her into an examination room. Dawn’s heart sped up as she stepped through the door marked “Conference Room A.” Inside, five chairs were set in a circle, like something out a Hollywood version of an Alcoholics Anonymous meeting. Dawn vaguely mused that the conference room was surprisingly devoid of the long conference table she had assumed would be the main piece of furniture in the room.
